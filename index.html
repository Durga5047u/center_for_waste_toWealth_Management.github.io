Center for Waste to Wealth Management

  • India currently generates 62 million tons of waste every year. The quantity of materials discarded can be reduced if they are used more efficiently. Owing to the lack of adequate waste management, the country is facing excessive environmental pollution which in turn affects public health. Sustainable waste management is the need of the hour.

Owing to the lack of adequate waste management, the country is facing excessive environmental pollution which in turn affects public health. Sustainable waste management is the need of the hour.
  • Currently a team of 14 members comprising faculty and research scholar from different disciplines such as Chemistry, Mecahnical Engineering, Chemical Engineering, Civil Engineering, Computer Science, Environmental Science, and Agricultural Science is actively involved in the waste management research and activities. The waste management is aligned with the 5 Rs - Rethink, Refuse, Reduce, Reuse, and Recycle and the Research Center for Waste-to-Wealth Management explores innovative waste management solutions to treat waste towards producing useful recycled materials, generate energy and create valuable resources. To achieve the same, under this center, the following different sub-units are in operation to convert the waste into wealth.
  • Centurion University believes that nothing is priceless and valueless. Everything including the waste generated from different uses can be converted to wealth. For the effective and sustainable waste management, the Research Center for Waste-to-Wealth Management is established at Centurion University of Technology and Management in the year 2020 with the following objectives.

  • Objectives

    • To explore and utilise different wastes and convert them into value added products
    • Create awareness in the community regarding different waste management
    • Research for producing the effective and efficient materials out of waste
    • To make the campus a zero waste campus
